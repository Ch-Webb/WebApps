Pre-Python Life

Van Rossum was born 31st January 1956. He grew up in the Netherlands, receiving a master's degree in computer science and mathematics from the University Of Amsterdam in 1982.
He worked at the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica, the US National Institute of Standards and Technology, and the Corporation for National Research Initiatives.

The Origin of Python

Around Christmas 1989, Van Rossum was looking for a programming project to occupy himself over the time his office was closed. He decided to write an interpreter for a new scripting language, that he described as a "Descendent of ABC that would appeal to Unix/C Hackers."[1]

All of this led to the creation of "Python", a programming language which has become one of the forefront technologies in the current programming world, with a download rate of about 34 million a year[2], as of 2013.
